Overview

The VR/AR and Imitation Learning Team develops cutting-edge extended reality (XR) solutions that bridge the gap between humans and robotic systems. Through immersive interfaces, we enable intuitive robot teleoperation, training, and collaboration in both virtual and mixed reality environments, transforming how humans interact with and control intelligent machines.

Research Focus

Our research encompasses virtual reality (VR) for robot teleoperation, augmented reality (AR) for real-time robot status visualization, mixed reality training environments, haptic feedback systems, and immersive simulation platforms. We focus on creating seamless XR experiences that enhance situational awareness, reduce cognitive load, and enable natural, intuitive control of complex robotic systems.

Team Members

Haoran Xie

Associate Professor

Dr. Haoran Xie received his Ph.D. in Computer Graphics from JAIST in 2015, and M.S. in 2012. He is currently an Associate Professor at JAIST and a Researcher (Associate Professor) at Waseda University through JST BOOST program. He directs the Human-Centered AI Laboratory, focusing on generative AI applications and human-AI interaction.

His research encompasses 2D/3D image generation, physical intelligence, and human-AI interaction, with notable achievements including the AniFaceDrawing project featured across major international outlets. He is a member of ACM, IEEE, IPSJ, and the Society for Art and Science.
